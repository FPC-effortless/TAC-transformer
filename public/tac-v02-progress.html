TAC v0.2

The next TAC milestone is a single scaling question: do persistent state, repair planning, and compression advantages survive at about 112M parameters on real language and code data?

What TAC Is

  • An experimental transformer variant with persistent identity state and routed program modules.
  • A research architecture for long-horizon agent memory, repair, and compressed context.

What TAC Proved In v0.1

  • Bounded persistent-state mechanisms can become causally useful.
  • 20x compression survived realistic local benchmarks.
  • Repair-control and causal-fix selection passed bounded sandbox tests.

What TAC Failed

  • Open-ended agency is not validated.
  • Long interacting repair chains still miss the completion gate.
  • v0.1 does not prove a transformer-beating scaling law.
